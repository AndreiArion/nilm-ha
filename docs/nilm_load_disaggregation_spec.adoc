== Purpose and scope

This document specifies *NILM-HA*, a Home Assistant custom integration that performs
_Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring_ (NILM, also called load disaggregation): from a **single
whole-house meter signal** (here: Linky téléinformation `PAPP`, apparent power in VA),
it automatically detects individual electrical consumers (fridge, water heater, kettle,
oven…), learns their signatures, and exposes per-appliance *power* and *energy* sensors
usable in the HA Energy dashboard.

The approach specified here is **classical event-based NILM** (Hart, 1992, "Nonintrusive
Appliance Load Monitoring"), chosen because:

* it works at the ~1–10 s sampling rate that a Linky TIC feed provides;
* it is fully explainable (every watt attributed can be traced to a detected edge);
* it is light enough (O(1) per sample) to run inside the HA event loop on a Raspberry Pi;
* it needs no labelled training data — it discovers clusters and asks the user to name them.

Out of scope for v1 (kept as roadmap items): factorial HMM disaggregation,
deep-learning seq2point models, per-phase three-phase support.

== Input signal: what we actually have

=== The Linky TIC `PAPP` feed

The screenshot driving this spec shows `CompteurElectric PAPP` — the *apparent power*
field of the Linky téléinformation (TIC). Facts that shape the whole design:

[cols="1,3", options="header"]
|===
| Property | Consequence for the algorithm

| Unit is *VA* (apparent power), not W
| Reactive loads (motors, SMPS) read higher than their real power. Fine for
  *detection* (edges are still edges), but energy attribution in kWh needs a
  power-factor correction or calibration per appliance (<<energy-attribution>>).

| Quantised to *integer VA*, and the meter itself rounds
| The signal is a staircase. Sub-~5 VA loads are invisible. Set the noise floor
  accordingly.

| TIC frames arrive every ~1–2 s (historic mode) or faster (standard mode),
  but *HA only records state changes*
| The recorded series is *irregularly sampled*: no new point while power is constant.
  The pipeline must resample to a uniform grid before windowed statistics
  (<<preprocessing>>).

| Single aggregate channel
| Simultaneous switching events overlap; the matcher must tolerate missed/merged
  edges (<<matching>>).
|===

=== Signal model

We model the aggregate signal as a sum of piecewise-constant appliance contributions
plus noise:

[latexmath]
++++
P(t) \;=\; b(t) \;+\; \sum_{a=1}^{A} s_a(t)\,\pi_a \;+\; \varepsilon(t)
++++

where stem:[b(t)] is the slowly-varying always-on baseline (router, standby…),
stem:[s_a(t) \in \{0,1\}] is the state of two-state appliance stem:[a],
stem:[\pi_a] its steady-state draw, and stem:[\varepsilon(t)] noise
(quantisation + small fluctuating loads). Multi-state appliances (washing machine)
are modelled as several coupled two-state components in v1.

The core consequence: *all information lives in the step discontinuities*
stem:[\Delta P] of the signal. The pipeline is therefore:

[[preprocessing]]
== Stage 1–2: Resampling and denoising

=== Uniform resampling

HA delivers `(t_i, x_i)` only on change. Rebuild a uniform grid with period
stem:[T] (choose stem:[T] = native TIC cadence, e.g. 2 s) by zero-order hold:

[latexmath]
++++
x[k] \;=\; x_{i}\quad \text{with } i = \max\{\,i : t_i \le kT\,\}
++++

Zero-order hold is *exact* here (the source only reports changes), unlike
interpolation which would smear edges.

=== Median filter

A short median filter kills single-sample glitches without rounding step edges
(a median preserves monotone steps — crucial, since edges are our signal):

[latexmath]
++++
\tilde{x}[k] = \operatorname{median}\big(x[k-1], x[k], x[k+1]\big)
++++

Window 3 (one sample of protection) is enough at 2 s cadence; use 5 at 1 s.
*Do not* use a moving average — it turns a step into a ramp and biases stem:[\Delta P].

== Stage 3–4: Event detection and feature extraction

Two complementary detectors run in parallel; an event is opened by either.

=== Steady-state segmentation (Hart)

Maintain a *steady-state* flag over a sliding window of stem:[n] samples
(stem:[n \approx 5], i.e. ~10 s):

[latexmath]
++++
\text{steady}[k] \iff \max_{j \in [k-n+1,k]} \tilde{x}[j] - \min_{j} \tilde{x}[j] < T_{ss}
++++

with stem:[T_{ss}] ≈ 15–25 VA for an integer-VA Linky feed. An *event* is the
transition steady → unsteady → steady; its magnitude is the difference of the
surrounding steady levels, estimated robustly as a difference of medians:

[latexmath]
++++
\Delta P \;=\; \operatorname{median}\big(\tilde{x}[k_{\text{after}}\,..\,k_{\text{after}}+w]\big)
          \;-\; \operatorname{median}\big(\tilde{x}[k_{\text{before}}-w\,..\,k_{\text{before}}]\big),
\qquad w \approx 5
++++

This is the estimator that gives clean stem:[\Delta P] even when the transition
contains a compressor inrush spike (the spike lies *between* the two windows).

=== CUSUM change-point detector

The two-sided CUSUM catches slow-onset changes the min–max test misses
(e.g. a heater with a soft-start), and gives a principled sensitivity knob.
With stem:[\hat\mu_k] the current steady-level estimate:

[latexmath]
++++
\begin{aligned}
g^{+}[k] &= \max\!\big(0,\; g^{+}[k-1] + \tilde{x}[k] - \hat\mu_k - \kappa\big)\\
g^{-}[k] &= \max\!\big(0,\; g^{-}[k-1] - \tilde{x}[k] + \hat\mu_k - \kappa\big)\\
\text{event} &\iff g^{+}[k] > h \;\lor\; g^{-}[k] > h
\end{aligned}
++++

Tuning (standard results from change-point theory):

* stem:[\kappa = \delta_{\min}/2] where stem:[\delta_{\min}] is the smallest jump you
  care about (e.g. 30 VA → stem:[\kappa = 15]);
* stem:[h \approx 4\sigma\text{–}5\sigma] where stem:[\sigma] is the steady-state noise
  std (measure it: for integer-VA Linky typically 3–8 VA). Larger stem:[h] = fewer
  false alarms, more detection delay (stem:[\approx h/\delta] samples).

After an alarm, freeze stem:[\hat\mu], wait for steadiness, re-estimate the new level,
reset stem:[g^{\pm}=0].

=== Transient (inrush) capture — the fridge signature

Your screenshot shows the classic refrigerator pattern: a *start spike*
(compressor locked-rotor inrush, brief overshoot above the new steady level)
followed by a flat plateau ~60–80 VA above baseline, repeating with a stable
period. We exploit the transient as a *feature*, not a nuisance:

.Fridge compressor cycle anatomy
[listing]
----
 VA
      inrush spike (0.5–2 s)
        ╱╲
       ╱  ╲___________________          <- plateau ΔP_ss ≈ +60..90 VA
       │                      │
 ______│                      │________ <- baseline
       ↑ ON edge              ↑ OFF edge (clean step down, no spike)
       |◀──── on-duration ────▶|
       |◀──────────── period (μ_Δ ≈ 30–60 min) ─────────▶| next ON
----

For every event, extract the feature vector:

[latexmath]
++++
\mathbf{f} = \Big(\;\Delta P_{ss},\;\; S = \max_{k \in \text{transient}} \tilde x[k] - P_{\text{after}},\;\;
\tau_{\text{rise}},\;\; \sigma_{\text{transient}}\;\Big)
++++

stem:[\Delta P_{ss}]:: steady-state step (signed, VA) — the primary feature.
stem:[S]:: spike overshoot above the settled level. Motors: large; resistive
  loads (kettle, heater): ≈ 0.
stem:[\tau_{\text{rise}}]:: samples from event start to settling
  (stem:[|\tilde x - P_{\text{after}}| < T_{ss}] for stem:[n] samples).
stem:[\sigma_{\text{transient}}]:: variability during the transition
  (distinguishes electronically controlled loads).

=== Reference implementation

[source,python]
----
from dataclasses import dataclass, field
from collections import deque
from statistics import median

@dataclass
class Event:
    t_start: float
    t_end: float
    dp: float            # ΔP_ss, signed VA
    spike: float         # inrush overshoot, VA
    rise_s: float        # settling time, s

@dataclass
class EdgeDetector:
    """Steady-state + CUSUM hybrid edge detector. O(1) per sample."""
    period: float = 2.0          # resample period T (s)
    t_ss: float = 20.0           # steadiness band (VA)
    kappa: float = 15.0          # CUSUM slack = δ_min / 2
    h: float = 30.0              # CUSUM threshold ≈ 4-5 σ
    win: int = 5                 # median window w

    _buf: deque = field(default_factory=lambda: deque(maxlen=64))
    _gp: float = 0.0
    _gm: float = 0.0
    _mu: float | None = None
    _in_event: bool = False
    _pre_level: float = 0.0
    _ev_t0: float = 0.0
    _ev_samples: list = field(default_factory=list)

    def _steady(self) -> bool:
        w = list(self._buf)[-self.win:]
        return len(w) == self.win and max(w) - min(w) < self.t_ss

    def push(self, t: float, x: float) -> Event | None:
        self._buf.append(x)
        if self._mu is None:
            if self._steady():
                self._mu = median(list(self._buf)[-self.win:])
            return None

        if not self._in_event:
            self._gp = max(0.0, self._gp + x - self._mu - self.kappa)
            self._gm = max(0.0, self._gm - x + self._mu - self.kappa)
            if self._gp > self.h or self._gm > self.h or not self._steady():
                self._in_event = True
                self._pre_level = self._mu
                self._ev_t0 = t
                self._ev_samples = [x]
            return None

        # inside an event: wait for the signal to settle again
        self._ev_samples.append(x)
        if self._steady():
            post = median(list(self._buf)[-self.win:])
            dp = post - self._pre_level
            peak = max(self._ev_samples) if dp > 0 else min(self._ev_samples)
            ev = Event(
                t_start=self._ev_t0, t_end=t, dp=dp,
                spike=max(0.0, (peak - post) if dp > 0 else (post - peak)),
                rise_s=t - self._ev_t0,
            )
            self._mu, self._gp, self._gm = post, 0.0, 0.0
            self._in_event = False
            # ignore micro-events below the noise floor
            return ev if abs(dp) >= 2 * self.kappa else None
        if len(self._ev_samples) > 300 / self.period:   # 5 min: give up, re-anchor
            self._mu, self._in_event = None, False
        return None
----

== Stage 5: Online clustering of signatures

=== Feature space and normalisation

Cluster ON events (stem:[\Delta P > 0]) and OFF events (stem:[\Delta P < 0]) separately.
Power spans two orders of magnitude (60 VA fridge → 3000 VA kettle+oven), and both
noise and appliance variability grow roughly proportionally with power, so work in
*log-magnitude* space:

[latexmath]
++++
\mathbf{z} = \Big( \log|\Delta P_{ss}|,\;\; \frac{S}{|\Delta P_{ss}|},\;\; \log(1+\tau_{\text{rise}}) \Big)
++++

The relative spike stem:[S/|\Delta P|] separates motor loads (fridge ≈ 0.3–2.0) from
resistive loads (≈ 0) at equal stem:[\Delta P] — this is what distinguishes a fridge
from, say, a 70 W lamp.

=== Bootstrap: DBSCAN over the event log

On first run (and nightly), cluster the accumulated event history with DBSCAN —
it needs no cluster count and marks one-off events as noise:

* stem:[\varepsilon = 0.15] in normalised stem:[\mathbf z] space
  (≈ ±15 % power tolerance, matching appliance draw variability with mains voltage);
* stem:[\text{minPts} = 4] — an appliance isn't a *pattern* until seen ~4 times.
  A fridge produces ~30–50 ON events/day, so it clusters within hours.

[source,python]
----
import numpy as np
from sklearn.cluster import DBSCAN

def cluster_events(events):                     # events: list[Event], same sign
    Z = np.array([[np.log(abs(e.dp)),
                   e.spike / abs(e.dp),
                   np.log1p(e.rise_s)] for e in events])
    Z = (Z - Z.mean(axis=0)) / (Z.std(axis=0) + 1e-9)
    return DBSCAN(eps=0.15 * np.sqrt(Z.shape[1]), min_samples=4).fit_predict(Z)
----

=== Steady state: online nearest-centroid assignment

Between re-clusterings, assign each new event to the nearest known cluster using a
per-cluster Gaussian model stem:[(\boldsymbol\mu_c, \boldsymbol\Sigma_c)] and a
Mahalanobis gate:

[latexmath]
++++
d^2_c(\mathbf z) = (\mathbf z - \boldsymbol\mu_c)^{\top} \boldsymbol\Sigma_c^{-1} (\mathbf z - \boldsymbol\mu_c),
\qquad \text{assign} \iff \min_c d^2_c < \chi^2_{3,\,0.99} \approx 11.34
++++

Events that pass the gate update the cluster with an exponential moving average
(stem:[\alpha = 0.05], so the model tracks seasonal drift — fridges draw more in
summer). Events that fail go to an *unassigned* buffer that feeds the nightly DBSCAN,
which is how *new appliances are discovered automatically*.

[[matching]]
== Stage 6: ON/OFF matching — cycles

Each ON-cluster is paired with the OFF-cluster of opposite, near-equal magnitude,
then individual events are matched into *cycles* by a per-appliance two-state FSM.

[plantuml, appliance-fsm, svg]
----
@startuml
[*] --> OFF
OFF --> ON : ON-edge from own cluster\n(ΔP⁺ within tolerance)
ON --> OFF : OFF-edge with |ΔP⁻| ≈ ΔP⁺\nand duration ∈ [d_min, d_max]
ON --> OFF : timeout d_max exceeded\n→ cycle discarded (low confidence)
note right of ON : accumulate energy\nΔP_ss × elapsed
@enduml
----

.ASCII fallback
[listing]
----
            ON-edge (cluster match)
   ┌─────┐ ─────────────────────────▶ ┌────┐
   │ OFF │                            │ ON │──▶ energy += ΔP·dt
   └─────┘ ◀───────────────────────── └────┘
            OFF-edge with |ΔP⁻| ≈ ΔP⁺,
            duration in [d_min, d_max]
----

=== Matching rule

An OFF event at stem:[t_2] closes the cycle opened by an ON event at stem:[t_1] iff:

[latexmath]
++++
\big|\Delta P^{+} + \Delta P^{-}\big| \;<\; \max\!\big(\epsilon_{\text{abs}},\; \epsilon_{\text{rel}}\,\Delta P^{+}\big)
\quad\land\quad d_{\min} \le t_2 - t_1 \le d_{\max}
++++

with defaults stem:[\epsilon_{\text{abs}} = 25] VA, stem:[\epsilon_{\text{rel}} = 0.12],
stem:[d_{\min} = 60] s, stem:[d_{\max}] = 6 h. When several pairings are possible
(overlapping appliances), solve greedily by ascending cost:

[latexmath]
++++
c(i,j) = \underbrace{\frac{|\Delta P_i^{+} + \Delta P_j^{-}|}{\Delta P_i^{+}}}_{\text{magnitude mismatch}}
\;+\; \lambda \underbrace{\Big|\log\frac{t_j - t_i}{\hat d_c}\Big|}_{\text{duration vs. cluster's typical }\hat d_c},
\qquad \lambda = 0.3
++++

Unmatched ON events expire at stem:[d_{\max}] and their energy falls into the
*remainder* sensor — the system never invents attribution it can't defend.

=== Handling simultaneous events

Two appliances switching within one settling window produce a single merged edge
stem:[\Delta P_1 + \Delta P_2]. v1 strategy: merged edges usually fail the cluster gate
and stay unassigned (correct behaviour — no false attribution). The nightly job
additionally attempts *edge decomposition*: test whether an unassigned
stem:[\Delta P \approx \boldsymbol\mu_{c_1} + \boldsymbol\mu_{c_2}] for known clusters
stem:[c_1, c_2] within combined tolerance, and if the corresponding OFF edges appear
separately later, retro-attribute.

== Stage 6b: Periodicity — auto-identifying the fridge

Cyclic thermostat-driven appliances (fridge, freezer, aquarium heater) are identified
*without any user input* from the statistics of their ON times stem:[\{t_i\}]:

[latexmath]
++++
\Delta_i = t_{i+1} - t_i, \qquad
\text{CV} = \frac{\operatorname{std}(\Delta_i)}{\operatorname{mean}(\Delta_i)}, \qquad
D = \frac{\operatorname{mean}(\text{on-duration})}{\operatorname{mean}(\Delta_i)}
++++

Classification heuristics (defaults, all configurable):

[cols="2,1,1,1,2", options="header"]
|===
| Pattern | stem:[\Delta P_{ss}] | CV | Duty stem:[D] | Verdict

| Compressor cycling | 40–250 VA | < 0.35 | 0.2–0.6 | *fridge/freezer* (label suggestion)
| High power, nightly, long | 1–3 kVA | < 0.2 (daily) | — | *water heater on off-peak (HC)*
| High power, short, irregular | 1–2.5 kVA | > 0.6 | — | *kettle / microwave*
| Motor spike + 30–120 min run | 300–800 VA | > 0.6 | — | *washing machine component*
|===

A robustness refinement: compute CV on the *median absolute deviation* rather than
std, and window the statistics per 24 h — fridge periods differ day/night (door
openings) but stay tight within a window. Optionally confirm periodicity with the
autocorrelation of the binarised state signal stem:[s_c[k]]: a periodic appliance
shows a clear secondary peak at lag stem:[\approx \mu_\Delta].

[[energy-attribution]]
== Stage 7: Energy attribution

Per closed cycle, attribute energy by rectangle rule (the plateau is flat by
construction — that's what "steady state" means):

[latexmath]
++++
E_{\text{cycle}} = \Delta P_{ss} \cdot (t_2 - t_1) \cdot \text{PF}_c \;/\; 3.6{\times}10^{6} \quad [\text{kWh}]
++++

`PF~c~` is a per-cluster power-factor estimate, default 1.0 (resistive), 0.8 for
clusters flagged as motor loads (spike ratio > 0.2). If the TIC also exposes an
energy index (`BASE`/`EAST`, Wh), calibrate: scale all stem:[\text{PF}_c] so that
stem:[\sum_a E_a + E_{\text{baseline}} + E_{\text{remainder}}] matches the index
delta over 24 h.

Baseline (always-on) is estimated as a rolling low quantile, robust to the fact that
the fridge is sometimes on:

[latexmath]
++++
b(t) = Q_{0.01}\big(\tilde x[k] : t - 24\text{h} \le kT \le t\big)
++++

and the remainder — the honesty metric of the whole system — is:

[latexmath]
++++
P_{\text{rest}}(t) = P(t) - b(t) - \sum_{c\,:\,\text{ON}} \Delta P_{ss,c}
++++

A healthy installation should see the remainder's daily energy share *decrease over
the first weeks* as clusters are discovered; it is exposed as a sensor and is the
primary quality KPI (<<validation>>).

== Home Assistant integration architecture

A pure-Python `custom_components/nilm/` integration, HACS-installable.
Only stdlib + numpy at runtime; scikit-learn is optional (the nightly DBSCAN has a
~40-line pure-numpy fallback since we cluster ≤ a few thousand 3-D points).

        self.clusters = ClusterModel.from_store(hass, entry.entry_id)

        self._last: float | None = None

    async def async_start(self) -> None:
        self._unsub_state = async_track_state_change_event(
            self.hass, [self.source], self._on_state)
        self._unsub_tick = async_track_time_interval(
            self.hass, self._tick, timedelta(seconds=self.period))

    @callback
    def _on_state(self, event: HaEvent) -> None:
        if (s := event.data.get("new_state")) and s.state not in ("unknown", "unavailable"):
            try:
                self._last = float(s.state)
            except ValueError:
                pass

    @callback
    def _tick(self, now) -> None:                       # zero-order hold resampler
        if self._last is None:
            return
        if ev := self.detector.push(now.timestamp(), self._last):
            cluster_id = self.clusters.assign(ev)       # None → unassigned buffer
            for update in self.matcher.feed(ev, cluster_id):
                self.async_notify_entities(update)      # power/energy/remainder
----

[[parameters]]
== Tunable parameters (defaults)

[cols="2,1,1,4", options="header"]
|===
| Parameter | Default | Unit | Meaning / how to tune

| `period` (T) | 2 | s | Resample grid. Set to native TIC cadence; never below it.
| `t_ss` | 20 | VA | Steadiness band. ≈ 3–4× steady-state noise std.
| `kappa` | 15 | VA | CUSUM slack = half the smallest jump of interest (30 VA).
| `h` | 30 | VA·samples | CUSUM threshold ≈ 4–5 σ. Raise if false events at night.
| `win` | 5 | samples | Median window for level estimation (10 s @ T=2 s).
| `eps` | 0.15 | — | DBSCAN radius in normalised space (≈ ±15 % power).
| `min_pts` | 4 | events | Occurrences before a cluster exists.
| `gate` | 11.34 | — | Mahalanobis χ²(3 dof, 99 %).
| `eps_abs`, `eps_rel` | 25, 0.12 | VA, — | ON/OFF magnitude match tolerance.
| `d_min`, `d_max` | 60, 21600 | s | Plausible cycle duration bounds.
| `alpha` | 0.05 | — | EMA rate for cluster drift tracking.
|===

The config flow exposes three presets (*sensitive / balanced / conservative*) that
scale `t_ss`, `kappa`, `h` together; the options flow exposes everything.

[[validation]]
== Validation plan

. *Offline replay first.* Export 2–4 weeks of `sensor.compteurelectric_papp` history
  to CSV; run `detector.py → clustering.py → matcher.py` as a plain script
  (`python -m nilm.replay history.csv`). Iterate on parameters where a run takes
  seconds, not days.
. *Ground truth for 2–3 appliances.* A single smart plug with power monitoring
  (e.g. on the fridge for one week) gives cycle-level truth. Metrics:
  event *precision/recall* (a detected ON within ±30 s of a true ON), and energy
  error stem:[|E_{\text{nilm}} - E_{\text{plug}}| / E_{\text{plug}}] (target < 15 %).
. *Conservation check (always on).* Daily:
  stem:[E_{\text{index}} = E_{\text{base}} + \sum_a E_a + E_{\text{rest}}] within 2 %
  (it is an identity up to PF calibration — deviation means a bug).
. *Remainder trend.* Remainder share of daily energy should drop below ~30 %
  after two weeks on a typical home; plot it on the dashboard.
. *Unit tests* on synthetic signals: single step, step+inrush, two overlapping
  appliances, missing samples, meter restart (state → `unavailable`).

== Known limitations (v1)

* *VA vs W*: attribution is in VA-derived kWh with a crude PF correction; absolute
  kWh per appliance carries ±10–20 % error until calibrated against the meter index.
* *Variable-power appliances* (induction hob, dimmed lights, heat-pump inverter — a
  ramp, not a step) are structurally invisible to an edge detector; they land in the
  remainder. This is the main motivation for the FHMM/deep-learning roadmap items.
* *Multi-state machines* (washing machine, dishwasher) appear as several independent
  clusters; grouping them into one logical appliance is a v2 feature
  (co-occurrence analysis of clusters within a 3 h window).
* *Merged simultaneous edges* are conservatively left unattributed (by design).
* Slow HA recording (minute-level, or a deadband upstream) degrades everything:
  inrush spikes vanish and small appliances blur. Fix the data feed before tuning.
